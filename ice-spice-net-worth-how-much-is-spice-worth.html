Early Life and Education

Ice Spice, also known as Isis Gaston, was born on January 1, 2000, in the Bronx, New York City. From a young age, she was exposed to hip hop music and was heavily influenced by artists such as Lil’ Kim, Pop Smoke, and Sheff G. Growing up in the Bronx, Ice Spice developed a deep appreciation for the local rap scene and the unique sound it produced.

Ice Spice attended school in the Bronx before transferring to a Catholic high school in Yonkers. Although she was academically inclined, Ice Spice’s passion for music eventually led her to drop out of college. She initially enrolled in SUNY Purchase but decided to focus on pursuing her music career full-time.

Despite her decision to leave college, Ice Spice’s education continued through her experiences in the music industry. She gained valuable knowledge and skills through collaborating with other artists and producers, and her dedication to honing her craft has been evident in the growth of her music.

Ice Spice’s Rapping Career

Ice Spice’s journey in the music industry began during her college years when she collaborated with music producer RiotUSA. The Evolution of Bronx Drill
