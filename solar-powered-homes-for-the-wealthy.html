The next trend in energy conservation is solar powered technology. Dwindling natural resources like oil has led to innovations in green energy. Let’s face it oil prices continue to rise to meet global demand, this trend will not end anytime soon.

Solar energy cells and panels work by absorbing the Sun’s energy. Silicon is mounted behind non reflective glass to produce photovoltaic (PV) panels that collect photons from the Sun and convert them into DC electrical power that flows into an inverter, the energy is then transformed into AC electrical power. This is a simplified explanation of how solar energy conversion works.

Photovoltaics (BIPV) have been used since the 1980's but only recently have they been used in large scale commercial and residential projects. United Solar is one such manufacturer of flexible thin film photovoltaic materials, this makes it able to integrate into the roofs and buildings of contemporary buildings. The future of solar technology lies in the integration with existing construction companies.

Concentrated Photovoltaic Solar Installations are projected to boom in the next few years. Spending on equipment for solar panels is expected to increase 30% to $3 billion in 2014, according to research firm IHS in Europe and the U.S.

The Middle East has become the hotspot for solar powered technology, Saudi Arabia intends to spend $100 billion to obtain a third of its future electricity from renewable sources by 2032.

Green thinking millionaires and billionaires are now incorporating solar powered technology into their homes. Today’s mansions and estates can be aesthetically beautiful and environmentally friendly. The expensive cost of the initial installation of solar panels will more than pay for itself  in less than 10 years. Once solar panels are installed they produce no greenhouse emissions. Current solar plans have a lifespan of 25 to 30 years, the next trend is recyclable solar panels composed of aluminum, glass and silicon in a photovoltaic solar panel.

With all the technical jargon covered, which houses in the world provide the best solar powered domicile? Check out the list, who knows, maybe solar powered living is something you would like to look into.

9. Luxury Orsos Island: $4.5 Million

Your own floatable private island home, a hybrid combination of luxury real estate and a luxury yacht.
