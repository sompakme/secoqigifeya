Jimmy Webb (born August 15, 1946) is an American musician, composer, and performer who is best known for his songs “Jimmy.” In addition to “Up, Up, and Away,” he has penned many platinum-selling masterpieces, including “By the Time I Get to Phoenix,” “Wichita Lineman,” “Galveston,” “The Worst That Could Happen,” “All I Know,” and “MacArthur Park.” He is also the author of the novel “Up, Up, and Away.” Several popular contemporary artists have covered his songs, including The 5th Dimension, Glen Campbell, The Supremes, Richard Harris, Johnny Maestro, Rod McKuen, Frank Sinatra, Thelma Houston, The Temptations, Barbra Streisand, Art Garfunkel, Joe Cocker, Judy Collins, Donna Summer, Linda Ronstadt, America, Amy Grant, Dionne Warwick, John Denver, Johnny Cash, James Taylor, Billy Joel, and Tom J. Sullivan. In 1986, Webb was inducted into the Songwriters Hall of Fame, and in 1990, he was inducted into the Nashville Songwriters Hall of Fame.

He was honoured with the National Academy of Songwriters Lifetime Achievement Award in 1993, the Songwriters Hall of Fame Johnny Mercer Award in 2003, the ASCAP “Voice of Music” Award in 2006, and the Ivor Novello Special International Award in 2012. He has also received the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award in 2013. His song “By the Time I Get to Phoenix,” according to the British Music Industry (BMI), was the third most played song in the fifty years between 1940 and 1990. Webb is the first musician in history to have won Grammy Awards for both music and lyrics, as well as for orchestration and orchestration.
