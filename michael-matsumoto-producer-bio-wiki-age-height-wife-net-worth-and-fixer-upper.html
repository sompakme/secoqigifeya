Michael Matsumoto Biography

Michael Matsumoto is an American producer. He gained fame for being the former executive producer for High Noon Entertainment, the production company for HGTV’s “Fixer Upper.” In 2008, he served as the producer of Syfy’s reality show ChaSe, based on the Japanese show Run for Money. His other series credits include HGTV Design Star, Top Shot, Dogs in the City, and Flipping Vegas.

Michael Matsumoto Fixer Upper

Matsumoto was featured on The hit series Fixer Upper. His house was designed by Chip and Joanna Gaines designed for him and his family. The family later decided to sell the house which was named one of the biggest transformations in the show’s history. Joanna Gaines once called it the biggest challenge of her time on the show.

During the show, the Gaineses took the run-down shack and transformed it into a beautiful farmhouse for Matsumoto. The house was particularly featured in the season 4 finale.

Michael Matsumoto House

Matsumoto’s house named “Little Shack on the Prairie” has four bedrooms and two bathrooms. The house was on the market for $400,000 in July 2019. Back in December 2018, the house was added to Airbnb for $350 per night, according to a report from Country Living.

The living area and kitchen are in one big open space, featuring exposed beams, industrial shelving, concrete countertops, and a waterfall island.  The master suite includes sliding barn doors and a raw wood ceiling made with salvaged wood from the home’s original structure.

While the master bathroom features two-toned tile floors, a sleek, clawfoot tub, and an antique-style vanity. For their daughters’ room, Joanna used a repurposed beadboard on the ceiling to give the room a whimsical feel. In the boys’ room, there’s corrugated metal wainscotting on the walls to give it a fun detail.

The house also has a tranquil back patio, which features black iron planters and an industrial-style light fixture that hangs above a perfect spot for a Harp Design Co. dining table.
