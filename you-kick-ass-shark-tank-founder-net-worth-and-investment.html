The Founders of You Kick Ass

You Kick Ass, the innovative company behind personalized superhero action figures, was founded by Keri Andrews and Alesia Glidewell. Together, they brought their unique talents and expertise to create a one-of-a-kind business that captured the imagination of customers worldwide.

Prior to launching You Kick Ass, Keri Andrews had an impressive background as a program manager at Microsoft. Armed with her strategic vision and business acumen, she played a pivotal role in shaping the company’s growth and success.

Alesia Glidewell, on the other hand, was already well-known in the action figure and animation industry. Her work as the voice and body model for the iconic video game characters in Portal and Portal 2 cemented her reputation and showcased her immense talent.

“We wanted to combine our passion for superheroes and 3D printing technology to create something truly unique,” Andrews explains. “We believed everyone should have the opportunity to become their own superhero.”

With a shared vision and a commitment to delivering top-notch products, Andrews and Glidewell embarked on their entrepreneurial journey to bring You Kick Ass to life. Their complementary skills and dedication to their craft enabled them to build a brand that resonated with customers far and wide.

Behind the scenes, You Kick Ass also had the invaluable contribution of Douglas Jordan, whose expertise in the industry played a significant role in the company’s early success.

The team at You Kick Ass continues to inspire and empower individuals by offering them the chance to become the superhero they’ve always admired. With Andrews and Glidewell at the helm, the company pushes the boundaries of what’s possible and sparks joy in the hearts of their customers.

Pioneering Personalized Superhero Action Figures

You Kick Ass shook up the toy industry by introducing personalized superhero action figures made possible through 3D printing technology. By leveraging cutting-edge techniques, You Kick Ass enables individuals to create their own custom action figures based on their likeness and imagination.

“We wanted to give people the power to express their unique identities and celebrate their inner superhero,” Glidewell explains. “Through our personalized action figures, we strive to inspire others to embrace their individuality and unleash their inner powers.”

“The ability to create a customized action figure that looks like you and embodies your alter ego is a game-changer,” adds Andrews. “It allows people to connect with their heroes on a whole new level.”

The journey of You Kick Ass is a testament to the power of entrepreneurship and ingenuity. By combining their passion for superheroes, their expertise in technology, and their commitment to excellence, Andrews and Glidewell have created a brand that continues to make a lasting impact.

Using advanced 3D printing technology, You Kick Ass meticulously transforms the submitted image into a custom action figure that closely resembles the likeness and features of the person. Each figure is carefully crafted with attention to detail, ensuring a truly personalized and high-quality product.

Creating your personalized action figure at You Kick Ass is simple and fun. Customers can choose a superhero name and even select a unique superpower for their action figure. You Kick Ass Shark Tank Pitch

During their appearance on Shark Tank, Keri Andrews and Alesia Glidewell presented their innovative company, You Kick Ass, and pitched for a $100,000 investment in exchange for a 10% ownership stake. Their captivating pitch centered around their personalized action figures, created using 3D modeling technology.

“Our mission is to give everyone the chance to become their very own superhero. With our cutting-edge 3D modeling technology, we can transform a simple photograph into a unique action figure that captures the essence of each individual.”

The You Kick Ass team emphasized the potential for these personalized superheroes to resonate with a wide audience, including fans of comic books, video games, and pop culture. With the investment, they aimed to streamline their production process and explore licensing agreements to expand their product range.

The Sharks were intrigued by the intersection of technology and personalization offered by You Kick Ass. They recognized the market potential and expressed enthusiasm about the possibilities for growth. The unique concept struck a chord with the Sharks, and they engaged in a lively discussion about the scalability of the business.

Ultimately, while Keri and Alesia left without securing a deal, their appearance on Shark Tank generated significant interest in their business and showcased the power of 3D modeling technology in creating personalized action figures.

The Outcome of You Kick Ass Shark Tank Pitch

After the successful pitch on Shark Tank, You Kick Ass founders Keri Andrews and Alesia Glidewell were thrilled to secure a deal with renowned investor Mark Cuban. Mark agreed to invest $100,000 for a 10% stake in You Kick Ass, recognizing the potential of the personalized superhero action figures produced using 3D printing technology.

“We were ecstatic when Mark Cuban expressed interest in our product. His investment would have propelled our business to new heights and opened doors to exciting opportunities,” said Keri Andrews.

However, despite the initial agreement, the deal with Mark Cuban ultimately fell through, resulting in a disappointing outcome for You Kick Ass. The inability to finalize the deal and secure the much-needed investment had a significant impact on the company’s future prospects.

Unfortunately, despite the initial success and recognition garnered from their appearance on Shark Tank, You Kick Ass faced numerous challenges and hurdles. The company was unable to sustain its operations and ceased operations a little over a year after the show aired.

The outcome of You Kick Ass’s Shark Tank pitch serves as a reminder of the unpredictable nature of entrepreneurship and the challenges startups often face, even with promising products or ideas.
